# **1. Blockchain Fundamentals**

### **Definition**

A blockchain is a *distributed, append-only, cryptographically secured ledger* updated only via consensus.
Key properties:

* Decentralized
* Immutable
* Transparent
* Secure (hashing, signatures)

### **Benefits**

* **Decentralization** â†’ no central authority
* **Transparency** â†’ trust between parties
* **Immutability** â†’ tamper-proof history
* **Security** â†’ cryptographic integrity
* **Availability** â†’ replicated across nodes

### **Limitations / Challenges**

* Scalability (low TPS)
* High energy usage (PoW)
* Privacy concerns
* Integration & cost barriers
* Smart contract vulnerabilities

### **Solutions**

* Layer 2 (rollups, channels)
* Sharding
* PoS replacing PoW
* Privacy solutions (ZKP, permissioned chains)

# **2. Blockchain Types**

### **Public-Permissionless (Bitcoin, Ethereum)**

* Open to anyone
* High decentralization
* Slow & lower privacy

### **Private-Permissioned (Hyperledger, Corda)**

* Restricted participation
* High throughput
* Suitable for enterprise

# **3. Consensus Mechanisms**

### **PoW**

* Miners solve puzzles
* Very secure
* High energy cost
* Defends strongly against Sybil attacks

### **PoS**

* Validators stake tokens
* Energy-efficient
* Supports scalability
* Used by Ethereum (post-Merge)

### **Why Consensus Matters**

* Prevents double spending
* Ensures all nodes share same ledger
* Removes need for central authority

---

# **4. Bitcoin Core Concepts**

### **Double Spending**

Prevented by:

* PoW consensus
* Blockchain immutability
* Confirmation depth

### **Merkle Trees**

* Efficient verification (SPV)
* Transaction integrity
* Compact block representation

### **Sybil Attack Defense**

PoW ties block creation to computational power, not identity.
