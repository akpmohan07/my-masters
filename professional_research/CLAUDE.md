## Overview

This module covers research ethics, legal frameworks (GDPR, IP law, AI regulation), logical reasoning, and ethical theory application. The assessment is essay-based, requiring structured arguments applying frameworks to real-world technology scenarios.

## Topics Covered

### 1. Ethical Foundations
- Morality vs ethics distinction
- Moral relativism vs cultural relativism
- Ethical theories: **Utilitarianism** (consequences), **Deontology** (duty/rights, informational autonomy), **Virtue Ethics** (character), **Social Contract Theory**

### 2. Logical Reasoning & Fallacies
- Structured argument writing: premises → conclusion
- Fallacies: ad hominem, appeal to authority, circular reasoning, Texas sharpshooter, anecdotal, correlation vs causation

### 3. Privacy
- Natural vs normative privacy
- Physical, decisional, and informational privacy dimensions
- **Contextual Integrity** (Nissenbaum framework) — information flows appropriate to social context
- Technology threats: data gathering, merging, mining
- Case studies: Cambridge Analytica, FBI v Apple encryption, Facebook/Instagram mental health leaks

### 4. Legal Frameworks
- **GDPR:** Principles, organisational obligations, Data Protection Officer (DPO) role, Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)
- **Max Schrems challenges:** Data transfer restrictions, Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- **US CLOUD Act** implications for EU data
- **Irish legislation:** Harassment Act 2020, Online Safety & Media Regulation Act 2022, Copyright and IP Law Provisions Act 2019
- **EU AI Act:** Risk-based regulation of artificial intelligence

### 5. Professional Practice
- ACM Code of Ethics
- Whistleblowing
- Defamation law
- Freedom of Information (FOI)
- ADAPT research canvas

## Exam Strategy (from StudyPlan.md)

**Tier 1 — Highest probability:** Privacy concepts, ethical theories, logical fallacies, data protection law, IP law, digital evidence.

**Tier 2 — Good ROI:** ADAPT canvas, ACM code, whistleblowing, defamation, FOI.

**Format:** Scenario-based essays requiring framework application (not just definitions). Emphasis on balancing competing ethical perspectives with structured arguments.
